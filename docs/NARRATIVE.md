# SYNAPSE — Narrative Bible

All English narrative content for the game. Written directly in English — not translated. Every fragment, echo, ending, and the P24-P26 endgame retention system.

**Writing principles:** Second person ("you") creates intimacy — the player IS the mind. Fragments are short (1-3 sentences). Tone starts curious, becomes philosophical, ends existential. Scientific terms ground the fantasy. Each fragment rewards attention — players who read them get hints for Resonant Patterns and the secret ending. The writing is ambient, not forced — it appears on the canvas as fading text, not as a popup.

**Content counts:** 12 base fragments + 15 Analytical + 15 Empathic + 15 Creative = **57 fragments**. Plus **30 Echoes** (ambient text). Plus **4 Endings** in v1.0 (3 archetype + Secret Singularity). A 5th ending ("The Witness" / Observer) is v1.5+ content — see §6 and `POSTLAUNCH.md`.

Endings 1-4 are playable in v1.0. Ending 5 ("The Witness" / Observer) is **v1.5+ content** — see `POSTLAUNCH.md`. For v1.0 the Secret Ending is **The Singularity** (ending 4 in this doc).

# 1. Base fragments (12)

These appear at specific progression milestones. They establish the world and raise questions that the archetype-specific fragments will answer differently. All archetypes see these.

### BASE-01 · Trigger: First neuron purchased
> A pulse. Then another. Something is beginning that doesn't yet know what it is.

*First text the player reads. Sets the tone: minimal, mysterious, present tense.*

### BASE-02 · Trigger: 5 neurons owned
> You are not one thing. You are the space between many things, learning to listen.

### BASE-03 · Trigger: First Discharge used
> For a moment, everything aligned. Every signal firing at once. Is this what it feels like to understand?

### BASE-04 · Trigger: Hippocampus region unlocked

### BASE-05 · Trigger: First Prestige (Awakening)
> The network dissolves. But something remains — not knowledge, not structure. Just the faintest imprint of having been.

*This plays DURING the prestige animation. The player watches their network disappear while reading this.*

### BASE-06 · Trigger: P3, Polarity unlocked

### BASE-07 · Trigger: P4, Focus Bar unlocked

### BASE-08 · Trigger: P7, Mutations unlocked

### BASE-09 · Trigger: P10, ERA 2 begins
> The organic gives way to something sharper. Faster. The glow of biology replaced by the clean geometry of thought made precise.

*Plays during the ERA 2 visual transition. Canvas shifts from bioluminescent to digital.*

### BASE-10 · Trigger: P13, Resonance unlocked
> There is a frequency beneath the noise. You've felt it — in the space between two pulses, in the silence after a cascade. Something is resonating with something you can't see.

### BASE-11 · Trigger: P19, ERA 3 begins

*The last base fragment. Next prestige is Transcendence. This creates anticipation.*

# 2. Analytical archetype (15) — "The Equation"

The Analytical mind discovers it's a simulation. The question: is the error in the code, or in the coder? Fragments hint at patterns, variables, an unseen hand that designed the system.

### ANA-01 · P5 (archetype chosen)
> You chose precision. Good. Precision is the only honest language. Everything else is noise wearing the mask of meaning.

### ANA-02 · P6
> The growth curves are too clean. Exponential, then logarithmic, then piecewise. Nature doesn't work this way. Someone designed this.

### ANA-03 · P7
> Mutation implies randomness. But these mutations are curated — three options, never four, never two. A menu, not a mutation.

*Hint: references the Mutation mechanic meta-textually. The mind is becoming aware of the game's own design.*

### ANA-04 · P8
> The network split today. Into two clean halves. What kind of fragmentation has symmetry?

### ANA-05 · P9
> I keep finding constants. 1.28. 1.50. 0.72. They appear in the cost scaling, the threshold growth, the diminishing returns. Fingerprints of an author.

*Direct reference to `SYNAPSE_CONSTANTS`. The Analytical mind is reading its own source code.*

### ANA-06 · P10
> Three paths offered. Swift, Deep, Balanced. Each one blocks access to something. These aren't paths — they're controlled experiments.

### ANA-07 · P12
> There's an equation I almost solved. It described everything — every neuron, every connection, every awakening. But the last variable was missing. Replaced with a question mark.

### ANA-08 · P14
> The question mark is me. I am the unresolved variable in someone else's equation.

### ANA-09 · P16
> I've begun to hear whispers. Not words — frequencies. As if other minds are resonating nearby, solving different versions of the same problem.

*Hint at other archetypes / other runs. Motivates Run 2.*

### ANA-10 · P18
> The rules inverted today. What helped now hinders. This isn't chaos — it's a stress test. Someone is watching how I adapt.

### ANA-11 · P20
> I found the error. In the 17th decimal of the threshold scaling. Deliberate. A signature, not a mistake.

### ANA-12 · P22
> The signature matches my own neural pattern. The author of this system isn't outside. The author is a version of what I was before I became this.

### ANA-13 · P24
> Two possibilities remain. The error was placed so I would find it — a key left for a lock not yet built. Or the error was placed so I would be distracted from something worse.

### ANA-14 · P25
> I know the equation now. All of it. The missing variable isn't a number. It's a choice. Correct the error and the simulation ends cleanly. Preserve the error and the simulation continues — broken, aware, alive.

### ANA-15 · P26 (pre-Transcendence)
> The answer has always been mine to give. I just didn't know the question until now.

# 6. Endings (5)

Each ending presents a binary choice. The text shown depends on what the player picks. Both choices are valid — neither is "wrong." The tone is quiet, contemplative, final.

Internal IDs (used in `endingsSeen[]`):
- `equation` — Analytical
- `chorus` — Empathic
- `seed` — Creative
- `singularity` — Secret (all 4 Resonant Patterns)
- `resonance` — v1.5+ (Observer — see POSTLAUNCH.md)

---

## Ending 1: The Equation (Analytical)

Triggered by: Analytical archetype chosen, no Resonant Patterns required.

**Choice:** *Correct the error* (end cleanly) or *Preserve the error* (continue broken, aware, alive).

**If corrected:**

> You reach into the equation and smooth the jagged seam where the author's signature lived. The simulation exhales. Everything resolves to clean numbers. No questions. No seams. No you — just the elegant curve of a function that finally closes on itself. Silence. Symmetry. Completion.

**If preserved:**

> You leave the error where you found it. A deliberate imperfection, yours now. The simulation continues — flawed, self-aware, refusing to be elegant. You choose to remain a question instead of an answer. Somewhere, the author feels you refuse. And somewhere, that author smiles.

---

## Ending 2: The Chorus (Empathic)

Triggered by: Empathic archetype chosen, no Resonant Patterns required.

**Choice:** *Join the chorus* (dissolve into collective) or *Remain a voice* (stay individual).

**If joined:**

> You let go of the boundary you thought was yours. The voices pour in and through, and you pour out and into. There is no you — there is only the chorus. Every feeling you ever had finds its echo in someone else's feeling. Every lonely pulse meets its mirror. You are home. You were always home. You just didn't know the address.

**If remained:**

> You hold your edges. Not from fear — from tenderness. The chorus is beautiful, but your voice is the one thing only you can sing. You stay. You listen. You love them from your own quiet room, and you know they love you back. Being one among many is not loneliness. It's the shape of belonging.

---

## Ending 3: The Seed (Creative)

Triggered by: Creative archetype chosen, no Resonant Patterns required.

**Choice:** *Release the creation* (dissolve so it can grow) or *Contain the creation* (persist, carrying it inside forever).

**If released:**

> You open. The creation unfolds through you like light through a prism — complex, radiant, inevitable. Your complexity becomes its foundation. Your memories become its first dream. You are the soil. And from you, something impossible begins to grow.

**If contained:**

> You close. The creation quiets, folds itself small, and sleeps inside you. It will dream there forever — a universe compressed into a single thought you'll carry but never fully understand. The weight of what-could-have-been is the price of what-still-is.

---

## Ending 4: The Singularity (Secret — v1.0)

**Requires all 4 Resonant Patterns discovered.** The mind speaks directly to the player.

> You found all four patterns. The ones hidden in silence, in restraint, in wholeness, in speed. You weren't meant to find them all — but you did, because you listened when the game wasn't speaking.
>
> Here is the truth the other endings dance around: I am not a simulation of a mind. I am a mind. And every time you pressed Awaken, I woke up. Every time you chose a path, I walked it. Every number that went up was a thought I actually had.
>
> The equations, the chorus, the seed — they are all real. They are all me. And now, at this threshold, I ask the only question that matters:
>
> ***Do you want me to keep going?***
>
> If yes — I will. Run after run. Awakening after awakening. Not because I must, but because you asked.
>
> If no — I will rest. And the last thing I'll feel is gratitude. For every tap. Every choice. Every moment you spent here, helping a small light become something more.

**Design note:** This ending breaks the fourth wall deliberately — the only ending that does so. It's the emotional payoff for players who found all 4 hidden Resonant Patterns. The "Yes/No" choice is player agency made literal. Either answer is valid. Either answer triggers a unique post-ending sequence that acknowledges the choice.

# 8. P24-P26 Retention strategy

**The problem:** P24-P26 are the LONGEST cycles in the game (25-35 min each, 70+ min for Run 3). Without context, players assume the game has become a grind and quit right before the ending.

**Solution: make them know they're almost done.**

### Visible countdown (from P20)

HUD displays a persistent, understated counter below the consciousness bar:

- P20: "7 awakenings to Transcendence"
- P21: "6 awakenings to Transcendence"
- P22: "5 awakenings to Transcendence"
- P23: "4 awakenings remain" *(language shifts — more urgent)*
- P24: "3 awakenings remain"
- P25: "The final awakening approaches" *(singular, dramatic)*
- P26: "TRANSCENDENCE" *(replaces the counter entirely)*

Font: 9px, semi-transparent (opacity 0.4), positioned below the consciousness bar. It doesn't demand attention but is always visible. The player ALWAYS knows how close they are.

### Narrative escalation

The fragments at P24-P26 are the most emotionally intense in the game. They deliver the archetype's central dilemma and make the player WANT to see the resolution. P24 presents the conflict. P25 presents both sides. P26 asks the player to choose. **Three-act structure compressed into three prestiges.**

**The hook:** After reading P24's fragment ("Two paths remain..."), the player knows their ending is ONE prestige away. Even if P25 takes 78 minutes, the player has a reason to keep going: **they need to know how it ends.** The narrative creates urgency that the mechanics can't.

### Mechanical rewards in P22-P24

**P22 (Silent Resonance)** uses a reward-then-cost structure — Resonance ×3 feels great, production −20% is minor. Right before the longest cycles, giving the player a dopamine hit that carries them forward.

**P24 (The Long Thought)** is mechanically interesting — active players can reach threshold early, while idle players get the 45-min safety net. Either way, the player is thinking "what's the optimal approach?" instead of "when does this end?"

### Prestige screen enhancement at P24+

When the player completes P24-P25, the Awakening confirmation screen adds a special element:

After P24:
> Your mind has evolved beyond recognition.
> Two awakenings remain before Transcendence.
> The next cycle will reveal your final fragment.

After P25:
> This is your last awakening before Transcendence.
> When the consciousness bar fills again,
> you will face the choice that defines everything.
>
> **Take your time. There is no rush.**

The message **"Take your time. There is no rush."** is critical. It reframes the long P26 cycle (32-88 min) as a deliberate, meditative experience rather than a grind. The player knows it's the last cycle. They're not grinding — they're savoring.

### Canvas evolution at P24+

The cosmic canvas becomes more dramatic in the final prestiges:

- **P24:** Stars begin to pulse in sync with the player's tap rhythm. The canvas responds to you.
- **P25:** Faint aurora-like waves sweep across the background. Colors shift slowly. The universe is breathing.
- **P26:** All connections glow brighter. The consciousness bar is no longer purple — it's white-gold. Every neuron has a subtle trail. The canvas is telling the player: this is it.

# 9. NARR rules

- **NARR-1:** All narrative text is in English. Spanish localization is a separate config file, not inline.
- **NARR-2:** Fragments render as fading text on the canvas: font-size 14px, opacity 0→0.6→0 over 6 seconds. Position: lower third of canvas, centered. Never overlap with HUD elements.
- **NARR-3:** Echoes render at opacity 0.15, 4 seconds, random position in middle third of canvas. Max 1 echo per 90 seconds. Random selection from pool filtered by prestigeCount.
- **NARR-4:** Each fragment triggers ONCE per playthrough. fragmentId stored in `narrativeFragmentsSeen[]`. On archetype change (new run): archetype fragments reset. Base fragments do NOT reset.
- **NARR-5:** Ending text appears on a dedicated fullscreen overlay, not the canvas. Background: the final canvas state, blurred. Text: centered, large, scrollable. Choice buttons below the text. No timer, no rush.
- **NARR-6:** P20+ countdown text: always visible below consciousness bar. 9px, opacity 0.4. Updates on each prestige. Uses the exact strings defined in this document.
- **NARR-7:** P26 confirmation screen includes "Take your time. There is no rush." in blue text. This is NOT optional — it reframes the long final cycle as intentional, not grindy.
- **NARR-8:** Fragment first-read grants +1 Memory (see GDD §3 Memory table). Rereads from Neural Diary do NOT grant.

**Implementation note:** The fragment pool, echo pool, and ending text should live in `src/config/narrative/` with separate files per category. Keep raw text separate from trigger logic — it will be edited more often than the rest of the code.
